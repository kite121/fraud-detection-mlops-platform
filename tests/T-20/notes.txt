Test Name: Fault tolerance - restart RabbitMQ and recover event-driven flows
Date: 27.04.26
Tester: Amir Valeev
Branch: fixed_final_branch
Environment: Local (Windows 11)

Goal:
- Verify that after restarting RabbitMQ, the platform recovers broker connectivity and continues to process both Celery training jobs and event-driven ingest listeners.

Preconditions:
- The platform is running in Docker.
- RabbitMQ is healthy before restart.
- The reconnect fix for custom broker consumers has been applied and ingestion-service rebuilt.

Steps performed:
1. Restarted RabbitMQ and verified broker startup logs and service status.
2. Triggered a new POST /train scenario after broker restart and confirmed worker-side recovery and successful job completion.
3. Rebuilt ingestion-service with the reconnect fix, restarted RabbitMQ again, then triggered a new POST /ingest and verified listener reconnection plus automatic data_ingested handling.

Expected result:
- RabbitMQ restarts successfully.
- Worker-service reconnects to the broker and continues processing new training jobs.
- ingestion-service background listener reconnects automatically after broker restart and continues handling data_ingested events.

Actual result:
- RabbitMQ restarted successfully and returned to a healthy/running state.
- worker-service temporarily lost the broker connection, retried automatically, reconnected, and successfully completed a new training job.
- The post-restart training job train_2790f6438361 completed successfully with model_version v010.
- After the reconnect fix, ingestion-service logs showed the data_ingested listener disconnecting, retrying with backoff, reconnecting, and then processing a new ingest event.
- The restored listener automatically detected drift and queued retraining job train_f5ae0258997d for batch 12.
